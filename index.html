Eden on Calgary Bay

The Artist

Hello — I'm Eden.
Let me introduce you to Mull.

I'm a printmaker and illustrator living and working on the Isle of Mull. My work is inspired by the rugged Scottish landscape and the quiet, wild moments that define life in the Hebrides.

"Whether it's an otter tucked into the seaweed at the water's edge or a Highland Cow wading through a mist-covered loch, every piece I create is rooted in the time I spend exploring this island."

After studying Illustration at Edinburgh University, I traded city life for wild coastlines, misty hills, and a studio in Pennyghael. When I'm not printing, you'll find me hiking through the hills or scrambling along the coast — gathering the inspiration that eventually becomes a carving on my workbench.

See how I work
🖨

Handmade, always

In a world of mass production, I believe in the beauty of the handmade. Every print is carved, inked, and pulled by hand — no two are ever identical.

🦦

Rooted in place

The Isle of Mull is not just where I work — it's what I work from. Every design begins with a real encounter on this island: an animal, a bay, a particular quality of light.

🌿

Made to last

I use archival-quality inks and papers so that your print will look beautiful for decades. These are objects made to be handed down, not disposed of.

The Island

Life at the edge
of the Hebrides

The Isle of Mull sits off the west coast of Scotland, in the Inner Hebrides. It's a place of extraordinary wildness — white-tailed eagles, playful otters, ancient standing stones, and coastline that changes colour with every tide.

I moved here after university and have never looked back. The light here is unlike anywhere else. It shifts constantly — from gold to slate to silver — and it makes you want to draw everything you see.

The Making

From island
to inked paper

Every print I make begins with a walk. The process from observation to finished print can take weeks — but that's what makes each one worth having.

Shop all prints About Eden

01

Explore &
observe

Every print begins not at a desk, but outdoors. I spend a lot of time hiking the hills and coastlines of Mull — watching the wildlife, sketching in notebooks, photographing the light at different times of day. When a scene or creature lodges itself in my mind and won't let go, that's when I know it's ready to become a print.

02

Draw &
carve

Back in the studio, I sketch the design and transfer it to a block of lino or wood. Then comes the carving — one of my favourite parts of the process. Using a set of specialist gouges, I carefully remove the material that won't print, revealing the image in relief. It's meditative, precise work, and a single block can take many hours.

03

Ink &
pull

Ink is rolled carefully and evenly across the surface of the block. A sheet of paper is laid on top, and the whole thing passes through my etching press. The pressure transfers the ink to the paper in a single, irreversible moment. For reduction prints like Calgary, this process is repeated multiple times in different colours — each layer revealing more of the final image.

04

A true
original

No two prints are ever identical. The hand-pressing process means that each one carries its own subtle variations in ink coverage, pressure, and texture. These are not imperfections — they are what makes each print a genuine original. Every piece is titled, signed, and numbered by hand before it leaves my studio.
